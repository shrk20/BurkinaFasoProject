What's the problem?

There is an estimated 75 to 199 million tons of plastic in our oceans today. Approximately 33 billion pounds of plastic enters our marine environment every year. Studies show that we could have more plastic than fish in the ocean by 2050. All of this plastic pollutes the ocean, and creates drastic and long-lasting problems that affect everyone. We need to step in now, before it’s too late.

Our Mission

We must reduce the amount of plastic in the oceans. We aim to do this by:

  • Organizing beach and river cleanups
  • Encourage and support the adoption of sustainable practices in everyday life, such as using reusable products, reducing plastic packaging, and participating in recycling programs.
  • Create and distribute informational materials, workshops, and online resources to educate the public on reducing plastic consumption and proper waste disposal methods.
  • Encourage and support the adoption of sustainable practices in everyday life, such as using reusable products, reducing plastic packaging, and participating in recycling programs.
  • Advocate for local, state, and national policies that support plastic reduction, recycling, and sustainable waste management.

How You Can Help

You can do a lot things to make a small or big difference:
Reduce single-use plastics,
Recycle properly and consistently,
Choose products which are packaged sustainably,
Support plastic-free initiatives,
Participate in beach cleanups,
Spread awareness!
Remember, no action taken is “too small”!
